Eligibility and benefits verification is one of the most important front-end RCM activities. Verifying a patient's coverage and financial responsibility before services are rendered prevents downstream denials, reduces write-offs, and improves the patient financial experience.

## What eligibility verification covers

Insurance eligibility verification confirms that a patient has active coverage with a specific payer on the date of service. Benefits verification goes further by identifying what services are covered, what the patient's financial responsibility will be, and whether any coverage limitations or exclusions apply.

Key information obtained during verification includes:

- Active coverage status and effective dates
- Copay, deductible, and coinsurance amounts
- Coverage limitations and exclusions
- Referral and prior authorization requirements
- Out-of-network benefits, if applicable

## Why verification matters

Claims denied for eligibility issues are among the most common and most preventable denial categories. When eligibility is not verified before service, practices risk:

- Full denial of the claim for inactive or incorrect coverage
- Patient responsibility not being collected at time of service
- Retroactive coverage changes that complicate billing
- write-offs from out-of-network services performed under in-network assumptions

## Verification workflow best practices

- Verify eligibility at least 24-48 hours before scheduled appointments
- Document verification results including reference numbers
- Communicate patient financial responsibility before the visit
- Re-verify for high-cost procedures and services
- Track eligibility-related denial rates to identify process gaps
