Prior authorization is one of the most time-consuming administrative tasks in healthcare. Each payer has unique requirements, forms, and processes, and failing to obtain proper authorization can result in full claim denial.

## Pre-submission steps

- Identify which services and procedures require prior authorization
- Verify authorization requirements with the patient's specific plan
- Gather required clinical documentation (notes, test results, imaging)
- Complete the payer-specific authorization form
- Submit via the payer's portal, fax, or phone line

## Follow-up steps

- Obtain an authorization reference number for every request
- Document the authorization details including approval dates and service limits
- Set expiration reminders to track authorization end dates
- Communicate authorization status to the scheduling team
- Follow up on pending requests within the payer's expected timeframe

## Common prior authorization challenges

**Missing documentation** is the most common reason for authorization delays. Payers often require specific clinical documentation that varies by procedure and plan.

**Authorization expiration** occurs when authorized services are not performed within the payer's validity window. Tracking expiration dates prevents denied claims from expired authorizations.

**Payer portal complexity** means each payer's portal works differently. Staff managing authorizations across multiple payers need organized access to each portal and clear documentation of authorization requirements.
