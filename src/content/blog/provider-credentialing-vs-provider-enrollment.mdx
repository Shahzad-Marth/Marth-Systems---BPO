The terms "credentialing" and "enrollment" are often used interchangeably, but they refer to two distinct processes in the provider lifecycle. Understanding the difference is important for practices onboarding new providers or maintaining network participation.

## Provider credentialing

Credentialing is the process of verifying a provider's qualifications, training, experience, and professional standing. This includes:

- Verification of medical education and residency training
- Board certification status
- State medical license verification
- Work history and professional references
- Malpractice insurance and claims history
- National Provider Identifier (NPI) and taxonomy
- DEA and state controlled substance registration

Credentialing is performed by hospitals, health plans, and credentialing verification organizations (CVOs) to ensure providers meet minimum standards for participation.

## Provider enrollment (payer enrollment)

Enrollment is the process of adding a provider to a health plan's network so the provider can receive reimbursement for covered services. This involves:

- Completing payer-specific enrollment applications
- Submitting required supporting documents
- Obtaining a payer identification number
- Adding the provider to the plan's provider directory
- Maintaining ongoing revalidation with each payer

Providers can be fully credentialed but still not able to bill certain payers if enrollment has not been completed.

## Key differences

| Aspect | Credentialing | Enrollment |
|--------|--------------|------------|
| Focus | Qualifications verification | Network participation |
| Performed by | Hospitals, health plans, CVOs | Individual payers |
| Outcome | Verified provider record | Payer ID for billing |
| Frequency | Initial + recredentialing (every 2-3 years) | Initial + revalidation (per payer schedule) |

## Why both matter

Without credentialing, a provider cannot join a hospital medical staff or health plan network. Without enrollment, a credentialed provider cannot submit claims to specific payers. Both processes must be completed for a provider to be operational and revenue-generating.
