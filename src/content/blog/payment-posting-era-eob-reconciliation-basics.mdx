Payment posting is a core back-end RCM function. It involves recording payments and adjustments received from payers and patients, reconciling them against claims, and identifying discrepancies such as underpayments.

## ERA vs EOB

**ERA** (Electronic Remittance Advice) is an electronic file sent by payers that contains payment and adjustment information in a standard format (ANSI 835). ERAs can be auto-posted into most practice management systems, reducing manual data entry and errors.

**EOB** (Explanation of Benefits) is a paper or PDF document that shows how a claim was processed, including allowed amounts, patient responsibility, and payments. EOBs require manual data entry and are more time-consuming to process.

## Payment posting workflow

1. Receive ERA or EOB from payer
2. Match the payment to the corresponding claim(s)
3. Post payment amounts to patient accounts
4. Apply contractual adjustments based on the payer fee schedule
5. Identify and flag underpayments
6. Post patient payments and adjustments
7. Reconcile posted amounts with the deposit
8. Report any discrepancies for follow-up

## Reconciliation

Reconciliation ensures that posted payments match actual deposits. Differences can arise from:

- Payer adjustments applied incorrectly
- Payments applied to wrong claims
- Bundled or split payments
- Patient payments posted to wrong accounts

Regular reconciliation identifies these issues early and keeps A/R accurate.

## Underpayment identification

Not all underpayments are flagged by practice management systems automatically. Identifying them requires comparing allowed amounts to contracted rates and fee schedules.
