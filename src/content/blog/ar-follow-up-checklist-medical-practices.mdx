Consistent accounts receivable follow-up is essential for maintaining healthy cash flow in any medical practice. Without a structured follow-up process, aging balances grow, underpayments go unaddressed, and write-offs increase.

Here is a practical A/R follow-up checklist for medical practices.

## Daily follow-up tasks

- Review new claim submissions for clearinghouse acceptance
- Process claim status inquiries for claims over 14 days old
- Respond to payer correspondence and requests for additional information

## Weekly follow-up tasks

- Run and review the A/R aging report by payer
- Follow up on claims in the 31-60 day aging bucket
- Identify and escalate underpayments on posted payments
- Review denial trends from the past week

## Monthly follow-up tasks

- Full A/R aging reconciliation
- Focused follow-up on the 61-90 day aging bucket
- Review and appeal high-value denials approaching timely filing limits
- Identify recurring denial patterns for root cause analysis
- Generate and review A/R performance metrics

## Quarterly clean-up tasks

- Deep clean of the 120+ day aging bucket
- Credit balance research and resolution
- Write-off review and policy compliance check
- A/R benchmark comparison against industry standards

## Key metrics to track

- Days in A/R (target: under 40 days)
- Percentage of A/R over 90 days (target: under 15%)
- Denial rate (target: under 5%)
- First-pass claim rate (target: over 90%)
- Clean claim rate (target: over 95%)
