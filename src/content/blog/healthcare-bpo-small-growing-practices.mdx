Small and growing medical practices face a unique challenge: they need the same revenue cycle and administrative support as larger organizations, but with tighter budgets and fewer internal resources. Healthcare BPO (business process outsourcing) offers a way to access experienced support teams without the overhead of hiring full-time staff.

## What healthcare BPO covers

Healthcare BPO for small practices typically includes:

- Patient registration and scheduling support
- Insurance eligibility and benefits verification
- Prior authorization coordination
- Charge entry and claim submission
- Payment posting and reconciliation
- Denial management and appeals
- A/R follow-up across all aging buckets
- Provider credentialing and enrollment
- Patient billing support

## Why small practices outsource

**Cost efficiency** is the primary driver. Outsourcing RCM and administrative functions converts fixed labor costs into variable costs, allowing practices to pay only for the support they need.

**Scalability** is equally important. A growing practice can add new providers, locations, and service lines without needing to recruit, hire, and train administrative staff.

**Expertise** is another factor. BPO teams that specialize in healthcare RCM bring experience across multiple payers, practice management systems, and workflows. Small practices get access to knowledge they would not have in-house.

## What to look for in a BPO partner

- Experience with your practice management system and EHR
- Clear processes and SOPs for each function
- Transparent reporting and communication
- Dedicated team members who learn your practice
- HIPAA-conscious workflows and data handling
- Flexible engagement that can scale as you grow

## Starting small

Many practices start by outsourcing one or two functions — such as A/R follow-up or eligibility verification — and expand the relationship over time as they see results.
