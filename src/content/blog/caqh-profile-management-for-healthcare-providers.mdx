The Coalition for Affordable Quality Healthcare (CAQH) operates a centralized provider data management platform used by most major health plans, hospitals, and credentialing organizations. A CAQH profile allows providers to maintain a single, standardized record of their credentials and practice information.

## What a CAQH profile contains

- Education and training history
- Board certification and licensure
- Work history and hospital affiliations
- Malpractice insurance coverage
- NPI, taxonomy codes, and DEA number
- Practice locations and contact information
- Reimbursement addresses

## Why CAQH profile management matters

Most payers require providers to have an active, current CAQH profile before they can be enrolled or revalidated in a network. When a provider's CAQH profile expires or contains outdated information, it can delay credentialing, revalidation, and reimbursement.

An expired CAQH attestation is one of the most common reasons for credentialing delays. Providers must re-attest to their profile accuracy every 120 days, and many payers will not proceed with enrollment until the attestation is current.

## Profile management best practices

- Set calendar reminders for 120-day re-attestation
- Update profile immediately after any practice or license change
- Review and respond to payer-specific requests for additional information
- Maintain digital copies of supporting documents for easy upload
- Monitor profile access logs to see which organizations have reviewed the data

## Common challenges

Providers often find CAQH profile management burdensome because it competes with clinical time. Keeping documents current, tracking expiration dates, and responding to payer inquiries requires ongoing administrative attention.
