Revenue cycle management is commonly divided into three phases: front-end, mid-cycle, and back-end. Understanding what each phase covers helps healthcare practices identify where bottlenecks and revenue leaks occur.

## Front-end RCM

Front-end RCM includes all activities that happen before or at the time of patient service. These steps are critical because errors made here often result in claim denials weeks later.

Key front-end activities include:

- Patient registration and demographics verification
- Insurance eligibility and benefits verification
- Referral coordination
- Prior authorization support
- Appointment scheduling support

When front-end data is accurate, downstream claim submission and payment processes run more smoothly. Incomplete or inaccurate front-end data is one of the most common sources of preventable denials.

## Mid-cycle RCM

Mid-cycle RCM covers the transition from clinical service to claim submission. This is where charges become claims, and accuracy at this stage directly impacts first-pass claim rates.

Key mid-cycle activities include:

- Charge entry from encounter forms and super-bills
- Coding review and modifier application
- Claim scrubbing to catch errors before submission
- Claims submission to clearinghouses and payers
- Clearinghouse rejection follow-up and correction

Practices that invest in clean claim submission processes see faster reimbursement and lower administrative overhead from claim rework.

## Back-end RCM

Back-end RCM covers everything that happens after a claim is submitted. This is where revenue is actually collected and where most RCM teams spend the majority of their time.

Key back-end activities include:

- Payment posting (ERA and EOB)
- Denial management and appeals
- A/R follow-up across all aging buckets
- Underpayment identification and resolution
- Patient billing and statement processing
- Aging AR cleanup
